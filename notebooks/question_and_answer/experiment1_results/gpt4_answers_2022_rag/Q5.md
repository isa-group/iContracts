# As a customer, can I modify the source code of the product or service to my liking?
## Background
The user question pertains to the ability of a customer to modify the source code of a product or service. This is a common question in the context of software licensing, where the terms of use are defined by the license agreement between the software provider and the customer. The ability to modify source code is typically addressed in the license grant and restrictions sections of such agreements.

In the provided context, GitHub's terms and conditions outline the rights and limitations of customers regarding the use of their products. Specifically, section 1.1 of the agreement grants the customer a nonexclusive and limited license to install and use the products ordered, subject to compliance with the agreement. Section 1.12 outlines restrictions on what the customer may not do with the products, including reverse engineering, decompiling, or disassembling any product, or trying to do so. These restrictions are standard in software licensing to protect the intellectual property of the software provider and to control the distribution and modification of the software.
